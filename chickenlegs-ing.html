Braised Chicken Legs
With Grapes and Fennel

Easy Cook Recipe

Ingredients
  • 4 chicken legs (thighand drumstick;
    about 3 1/2 lb.), patted dry
  • 4 1/2 tsp. kosher salt, divided
  • 2 tbsp. extra-virgin olive oil, divided
  • 2 medium fennel bulbs (about 1 lb.) plus
    1/4 coarsely chopped fronds
  • 1 cup unsalted chicken broth
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 4 tsp. Calabrian chile paste or chopped Calabrian chiles
  • 1 tbsp. red wine vinegar
    Procedures
  • 1

    Preheat the oven to 375Â°F and place a rack in the middle.

  • 2

    Season the chicken legs all over with 3 tsp. of koser salt.




  • 3

    Heat 1 tbsp.of olive oil in a large Dutch oven or heavy pot over medium-high heat.

  • 4

    Place 2 chicken legs, skin side down,in the pot and cook until golden brown, about 5 minutes



  • 5

    Flip the chicken legs and cook until golden on the other side, about 2 minutes. Transfer to a plate and repeat with the remaining chicken legs.


  • 6

    Meanwhile, prepare the fennel by slicing it into 1/2 thick slices, discarding the root end and tougher center from each bulb.





  • 7

    In a large bowl, combine the sliced fennel, half of the grapes, 1/2 tsp. salt, and 1 tbsp. of olive oil. Toss to coat and set aside.







  • 8

    Reduce the heat to medium and combine the chicken broth, honey chile paste, and remaining 1 tsp. salt in the pot Stir to dissolve the salt.







  • 9

    Add the remaining grapes to the pot and arrange the chicken legs, skin side up on top of the grapes.





  • 10

    Top with the dressed fennel and grapes. Don't worry if it looks crowded, as the fennel will soften as it cooks.





  • 11

    Transfer the pot, uncovered, to the preheated oven and roast for 30-40 minutes, or until the fennel is slightly softened, the grapes on the top browned and the chickenis cooked through.









  • 12

    Allow the dish to cool for 5 minutes before serving.




  • 13

    Using the slotted spoon or tongs, transfer the grapes and fennel to a separate bowl and place the chicken on a platter.





  • 14

    Return to the pot to medium-high heat and bring the braising liquid to simmer. Cook until reduced by half, about 8-10 minutes. Remove from heat and stir in the red wine vinegar and fennel fronds.









  • 15

    Spoon the sauce over the chicken to serve with the fennel and grapes alongside.

When it comes to cooking chicken, there are countless ways to infuse it with flavor. One particularly rustic and flavorful method is braising the chicken in wine. However, in this recipe, we take unique approach by using grapes as substitute for wine, resulting in a dish that is both sweet and tangy.
